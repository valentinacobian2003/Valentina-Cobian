Just like every B2B company ran Hubspot’s SEO content strategy in 2017, every B2B company should be running this dark social content strategy in 2024 👇 

1. Host a weekly live event where you provide free consulting, advice, or information to your target customers (current & prospective).

2. Convert the recording of that live event to a long-form podcast (Apple Podcast, Spotify) and long-form video podcast (YouTube, Website). 

3. Cut the long-form video for micro-content for social media platforms (LinkedIn, YouTube shorts, Tik Tok, Facebook Reels, etc.)

4. Monitor performance of organic social media posts, and used paid LinkedIn to amplify your best performing posts to your target account list (Tier 1, Tier 2, or both). 

5. Collect quantitative & qualitative insights from all of these activities (Event chat, social media comments, dark social feedback, etc.) that drives future topics & ideas for the next event. 

6. Measure success against Total Pipeline generated and Blended Marketing ROI. 

7. Repeat, Learn, Iterate.

Fun fact: My worst performing LinkedIn posts drive the most revenue. 
 
Here’s why:
 
1. My “worst” performing posts aren’t because the content is bad. It’s because I’m sharing new thinking that isn’t yet widely accepted. These posts don’t get a lot of likes, but instead they challenge established thinking and spark curiosity. 
 
2. My “best” performing posts usually perpetuate existing, established thinking. People clap and cheer in support of the ideas they already believe. These posts get a lot of likes and views, but don’t challenge new thinking. 
 
This same effect happened 3 years ago when I started posting about lead gen vs. demand gen. Nobody believed this thinking yet, and they were my worst performing posts for "engagement", but my best performing posts to drive pipeline & revenue. 
 
_
 
My post yesterday was my “worst” performing post since August 2021 (205 likes, 25,000 views). 
 
Yesterday, I also had 3 C-Level execs DM me to explore working with my company Refine Labs (most qualified inbound DMs in 1 day in the past 12 months). 
 
Stop trying to hack the algorithm for likes and engagement. 
 
Start sharing radical, non-obvious thinking that challenges established, widely accepted norms that don’t make sense anymore.
