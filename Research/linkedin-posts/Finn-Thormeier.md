Date: February 2025 (approx)
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/finnthormeier_how-do-you-know-linkedin-is-working-for-you-activity-7287824054579822592-mR-_?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_ios&rcm=ACoAADVDl30BFFnak4fNuobO-66pqRkE3P_9C9g

How do you know Linkedin is working for you? Welllll... Here's 3 interesting things about our Self-Reported Attribution (SRA) data that I started noticing recently:

1. It used to be 90%+ Linkedin, now it's more like 70% while other sources show up more. The main ones being referrals, communities (like Pavilion), and our own podcast

2. When it comes to referrals, some of it is our own customers, but surprisingly often it's someone in the industry who has heard good things about us or who has been following my content for a while I call this "non-customer referrals" and it's one of the cool side-effects of sharing your expertise publicly for a long time. I see it as a good indicator that your content is generating trust, if a non-customer feels comfortable sending people your way.

3. Let's say someone said "Linkedin" in SRA, even 1 or 2 days after booking the demo, people often don't remember anymore if what they saw was a Linkedin post by me, a Linkedin ad, someone else's post mentioning me, my comment on some other post, etc - so it all gets lumped into one.

There is no way around asking people again on the call and digging into it. I often refresh their memory with the date when they booked the demo, which often helps them remember. 

For example, for a recent prospects, after searching through my recent posts, he remembered that it was the one on the top 5 trends from talking to 30+ SaaS CMOs, CROs and CEOs that I posted last week that made him reach out. Super valuable info.

That's also how I found out that Tim Davidson had posted about me a month ago. He never tagged me, so I only found out yesterday through the convo with the Head of Marketing. (I linked both these posts below)

The takeaway for me:

Add value. Keep showing up. Share your best ideas & playbooks freely. Comment on other people's stuff. Engage in the community. Keep doing this consistently for a long time and good stuff will happen. It's a game of compounding.

## Post 2
Date: September 2025 (approx)
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/finnthormeier_if-i-was-head-of-brandcommspr-at-a-b2b-activity-7359179259543703554-44Vb?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_ios&rcm=ACoAADVDl30BFFnak4fNuobO-66pqRkE3P_9C9g

If I was Head of Brand/Comms/PR at a B2B tech company doing $10M-100M ARR, my #1 priority would be launching an Executive Linkedin Strategy. Here's how I'd get it done:

Posting company announcements is table stakes. And boring. 

My goal is to create interesting thought leadership content that gives us a competitive edge.

Here's how:

1. Identify 3-4 executives

I definitely want my founder/CEO. Who the other people are depends on our market and buyer, could be our CRO, CTO, certain VPs/Directors who are SMEs. I’d prioritize whoever has strong opinions and is willing to spend the 1-2h/week this will take

2. Content engine

- Schedule bi-weekly 30-60min meetings with each of my execs
- Podcast-style interview to extract their insights, opinions and industry commentary
- Zoom + Fathom to record, transcribe and summarize interviews. If we want video clips, use Riverside
- Use AI to draft Linkedin posts based on the interview transcript that are 80% done. I’d polish, shape and rewrite to get it to 90-95%
- Send over to exec to review & approve. I’d focus on text-only posts to simplify approval loops (and they still crush if you look at eg. Gal Aga)
- Publish 2x per week per executive, so 4 posts per interview

3. The right interviewer

You need someone with the following skills

- Good interviewer: Look for someone who has/had a podcast
- Business acumen: Don’t throw a fresh college graduate on your senior execs or you’ll end up with fluffy TikTok-style content
- Understands the space: They need a good understanding of your company, product, industry and ICP – so they can ask good follow-up questions 
- Worked directly with senior execs: founders/execs are hard to work with. Opinionated, high expectations, little patience. They need to handle it
- Content strategy: They need to know copywriting and Linkedin inside out. Hooks, formats, what works, how to write concisely

If this sounds like a difficult person to find, I agree. Shoot me a DM and I’ll help ;)

4. Connect my executive with relevant stakeholders

I’d build a list of the most important people my CEO and execs should be connected with (C-suite at Tier 1 target accounts, customers, partners) and send out up to 75 connection requests per week to strategically expand their network

5. Thought Leader Ads

I’d allocate a small budget (eg $5k/month) to make sure that our most important messages and posts get seen by EVERY single relevant stakeholder we want to reach

6. Enable Sales & Marketing to harness the profiles

- Thought leadership is great for awareness, PR, building credibility in our segment, but a couple months into it, the exec profiles will also become a goldmine for our GTM team
- I’d work with RevOps to use tools like Fibbler, Trigify.io, n8n and Clay to push our ad and organic post engagements into CRM and use it to score accounts
- Sales can reach out to warm prospects, Marketing can see in real-time which messages resonate

## Post 3
Date: March 2026 (approx)
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/finnthormeier_if-i-was-running-marketing-at-an-enterprise-activity-7429484763284992000-aS5y?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_ios&rcm=ACoAADVDl30BFFnak4fNuobO-66pqRkE3P_9C9g


If I was running marketing at an enterprise SaaS (100k+ ACVs) doing $5m+ ARR, where my CEO was still heavily involved in every deal, my #1 priority would be helping them transition to a scaled thought leadership motion:

let's talk about why first:

- CEO sells best, but it doesn't scale. Sales is inherently 1-to-1 and you can only take so many calls in a day

- Your sales team still needs your authority though: When you hire AEs and remove yourself from sales, win rates drop off a cliff. Your title carries a lot of weight, you deeply understand your market and customers - that's why you can sell so well. A good strategy allows you to put out thought leadership content that builds trust & credibility at scale to provide your sales team with air cover

To the how:

1. Evaluate CEO/audience fit

The goal is to become your ICP's #1 resource. So the CEO needs to have relevant insights for your specific ICP. If you sell to VPs of Engineering, the founder needs to have deep technical expertise. In some cases, someone else besides the CEO is best suited for this

2. Create a content engine

To create weekly bite-sized Linkedin content I'd create a longform "source material" piece, going 1 of 2 routes:

- Host a weekly live event where the founder shares what they are currently seeing in the market and/or interviews a customer/thought leader in the space
- Have someone else at the company interview the founder once a week around the company, product updates, their expertise, what they're hearing from prospects, eg. using Riverside.fm

3. Focus on quality > quantity

Everyone tells you to post daily on Linkedin. I'd go the opposite route: it's gotten so crowded that only the BEST content wins. I'd rather create 2 or 3 BANGER posts per week that are deeply insightful than post 7x fluffy, trite thing about work-life balance

4. Analyze what works & double down

I'd use a tool like Shield to track which posts performed the best. Once I have a "winner" (top 5-10%), I'd leverage them in a couple ways:

- Do more posts around that same topic because it clearly resonated
- Run those winners as TL ads towards target accounts
- Repurpose the topic into a blog post, podcast, webinar, etc
- Use that messaging in our outbound and on our website

5. Push ICP engagers into Clay, enrich, filter by ICP and score by engagement, then have the sales team run warm outbound towards them (lots of details here, ran out of space - DM me if you want a detailed breakdown)

Now we have a smooth engine to leverage the CEO's authority and expertise to provide air cover to the sales team and create trust and demand in our market at scale.
