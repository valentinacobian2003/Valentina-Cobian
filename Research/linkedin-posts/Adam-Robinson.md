I spent the last 18 months pursuing content-market fit. 

It clicked on Labor Day of last year.

At that point, all I was doing was posting on LinkedIn.

(I also made a docuseries, which no one watched)

Since then I’ve been putting the pieces in place to: 

1. Become even more “known well” (vs. well known) 
2. Create the perception that I’m literally everywhere
3. Get in front of OTHER PEOPLE’s superfans

I’m trying to become more “known well” by:

- Integrating more narrative into my LinkedIn
- Doing my weekly live webinar
- Sending out a weekly newsletter
- Doing more talking head videos 
- Creating another season of Billion Dollar Challenge
- Using AudiencePlus and podcast platforms to give people a “Binge Bin”

I’m trying to create the perception that I’m literally everywhere by: 

- Getting on everybody else’s podcasts 
- I’ll do literally anyone’s show, so long as you are B2B GTM (DM me!)
- Doing keynotes at major industry events 

And I’m trying to get in front of OTHER PEOPLE’s superfans by: 

- Getting on other people’s podcasts

Why the heck am I doing all of this? 

I believe we’re in a new era of Go-To-Market. 

Traditional outbound channels are dying a painful death. 

Response rates are the main culprit.

What will keep working as response rates continue to drop? 

Building Human-to-Human connection at scale, with organic social media.

The future of Go-To-Market is already here. 

It’s just not evenly distributed.

## Post 3
Date: May 2025 (approx)
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/retentionadam_going-viral-on-linkedin-can-be-worth-millions-activity-7319412722071150592-EAce?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_ios&rcm=ACoAADVDl30BFFnak4fNuobO-66pqRkE3P_9C9g

Going viral on Linkedin can be worth millions. Most people think it's luck. But in B2B, the best startups are engineering it. I’ve done it 50 times in the past year. Here’s the 5-step system I use to go viral on Linkedin every week—and turn attention into ARR:

1. Build in Public

But not just any build in public. Share things like full P&L, problems in your business, emotional agony, cease and desist letters… Things you would only share with your spouse behind closed doors. It will crush.

2. Credibility Posting

"I bootstrapped my startup from $1-$10m ARR in 14mo with 6 people…” Have you done something virtually no one else has? Post about it. Tell people up front why they should listen to you.

3. Failure Posting

Very few are willing to post about mistakes they’ve made on LinkedIn - they’re trying to look smart and successful. What they don’t understand is vulnerability builds more trust. Lean in to your shit and it will cut through.

4. Hot Takes

What strong, contrarian opinion do you have that is authentic to you but will FOR SURE piss off 25-50% of the people who read it? Write about it. If it goes viral, make it a content pillar.

5. Shitpost

This is my LEAST favorite but can be a valuable weapon when the time is right. Go STRAIGHT AT a larger competitor, and don’t stop until LinkedIn stops rewarding you for it. Fortune favors the bold with this one. It can also backfire, so pick your shots.

TAKEAWAY:

Going viral on LinkedIn - ESPECIALLY within your ICP - can be worth millions.

Just look at RB2B… 

We went $0-$5m ARR in 12mo with 6 people. 

But here’s the thing… 

Breaking through the noise, even once, is not easy. 

CONSISTENTLY going viral is even harder.

But if you can do it… 

You will possess the greatest power in the world today.

Attention on-demand.
