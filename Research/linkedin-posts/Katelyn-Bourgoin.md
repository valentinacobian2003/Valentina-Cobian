## Post 2
Date: February 2026 (approx)

Most content advice only works for wannabe influencers. If you need leads—not just likes—try this instead:

—
I’ve helped 1,800+ entrepreneurs build personal brands. Many became dominant voices in their space (and their sales exploded in the process).

Yet I’ve also watched qualified experts remain painfully invisible.

I bet you’re excellent at what you do, but your content doesn’t drive pipeline.

(And, no. You don’t need to “hack the algorithm” or spend hours per day commenting.)

What you need is content that makes your expertise unignorable—so clients come to you, cash in hand.

That’s what I call ‘authority content’.

And there’s a simple equation for creating it:

—
Authority =
(Problem + POV + Promise + Proof)
× Repetition

Each element is backed by behavioral science and maps directly to your buyer’s journey.

Here’s how it all works together:

—
PROBLEM — Name their struggle

Example: “You don’t have a lead gen problem. You have a positioning problem.”

↳ The science: Problems activate survival mechanisms (fear/urgency) and when you articulate their problem better than they can, they lean in

↳ Buyer stage: Attracts new people who recognize the problem

—
➕ POV — Show your differentiated thinking

Example: “Most content advice only works for wannabe influencers. If you wanna get leads—not just likes—try this instead:” (Look familiar?)

↳ The science: Sharing your contrarian beliefs, proprietary frameworks, and ownable ideas triggers the von Restorff effect (you stand out) while challenging best practices signals status

↳ Buyer stage: Engages problem-aware people seeking fresh perspectives

—
➕ PROMISE — Tease what’s possible

Example: “I used to work 80 hours a week just to scrape by. Now I average 5 hours per day and had my best year yet. What changed?”

↳ The science: Triggers social comparison and self-efficacy while tapping into identity aspiration (we want to become who we admire)

↳ Buyer stage: Converts lurkers into believers

—
➕ PROOF — Build credibility

Example: “My client went from stuck at $200k for years to doing $600k in 9 months—same service, better positioning.”

↳ The science: Social proof and authority heuristic bypass skepticism to build trust

↳ Buyer stage: Converts believers into buyers

—
✖️ Repetition — The multiplier

Clarify your messageing and then repeat key talking points consistently.

↳ The science: repetition builds familiarity and trust. And the more we hear an idea, the more we come to like it. 

↳ Buyer stage: Reinforces recognition across the entire buying journey

🟰 Authority 

—
I’m no mathematician, but I know this:

Strategy beats volume. Every time.

Once you understand the authority equation, you’ll stop posting randomly and start building a reputation that commands premium pricing.

Your competitors might post more. But you’ll be the one clients seek out.

## Post 3
Date: March 2026 (approx)

“Your audience doesn’t need to be large to be lucrative.” ~ Me

This is a slide from Lesson 1 in the original Unignorable Challenge. I still believe it. But it’s not the full story.

That original video lesson starts with a cautionary tale:

Arii had 2.6 million Instagram followers. But when she launched her clothing line, she couldn’t even sell 36 t-shirts. woof

Now you could use that story to argue big audiences are inherently hollow. Wrong lesson.

Arii’s problem was that she built her audience before she knew what her business was. Millions of followers, no real strategy. Just chasing the algorithm for growth at all cost. When it came time to sell something, she was known but wasn’t trusted.

Now look at GaryVee. Gary built a massive and broad audience, yet it worked for him. His original business was a social media agency, so his following was his proof of concept. It gave him leverage you can’t buy, which he used to build a $250M agency.

And look at April Dunford—big audience built on the back of a suuuuuper narrow topic: positioning for B2B software companies.

Some followers will hire her. Most won’t. But she’s found a way to make her narrow topic incredibly engaging, which drives huge word-of-mouth.

(Her toilet stories are riveting).

Building big audiences amplified Gary and April’s businesses… without diluting their ideas.

Meanwhile, LinkedIn is full of people building big followings on constant memes, hustle porn, and rage bait. The engagement is real. The authority isn’t.

I’ve built mine differently…

I have 280K followers across social and my newsletter. It helped me make millions from ads and courses. And as I’ve shifted to consulting, I my content shifted too.

Now I have more inbound than I can handle and a growing waitlist.

Different goals, different content strategy. Both approaches worked—because both were intentional.

The debate was never big vs. small.

It’s always been: built on what?

A targeted 3K can be a goldmine.
A strategic 500K can build an empire.

And a sloppy 2.6M can leave you unable to sell 36 shirts

Agree?
