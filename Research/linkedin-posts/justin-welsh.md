## Post 1
Date: October 2025 (approx)
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_i-cracked-linkedins-code-in-8-moves-783895-activity-7374771336491008000-W2Fy

I cracked LinkedIn's code in 8 moves.

783,895 followers later, here's the playbook:

1. Write about your journey, not your expertise

People follow stories, not resumes. I shared my burnout, my failures, my $70M walk-away. Vulnerability beats authority every time.

2. Repurpose your best content regularly

My top 21 posts have generated 6.384M impressions, and 90% of them were posted before. Think like a band: play your hits often.

3. Create a signature framework people talk about

"The LinkedIn Operating System" became bigger than me. When 45,000+ people use your framework, you've built a movement.

4. Solve one expensive problem repeatedly

With every post, I try to answer: "How do I build attention or revenue on my own?" That's a $100K+ problem. Pick one expensive pain point and become the best solution.

5. Turn your weaknesses into your brand

I got fired 3 times and made it my origin story. Your biggest embarrassments are your best content. People connect with flaws, not perfection.

6. Build in public with real numbers

"$10.8M business." "783,895 followers." "2,119 straight days." Specificity creates trust. Vague claims create doubt.

7. Focus on one platform until it hurts

I ignored Instagram, Twitter, and everything else. Just LinkedIn for 2.5 years straight. Depth beats width until you hit critical mass.

8. Document the system, not the success

Nobody cares that you made $1M. They care HOW you made $1M. Share the process, not the prize. Systems scale. Success stories don't.

Building a following AND a business here is not easy. A lot of people have the first without the second. You can't be focused on hacks and tricks. 

"Will using ↳ make my posts good?" No. That's silly stuff. Learn to write and educate.

"What's up with the algorithm?!" It changes. That's why fundamentals win over time. Complaining about it doesn't help you.

If you want to understand why 45,000+ people and over 100 of LinkedIn's Top Voices are students of mine, come see the system. 

It's deep education building a brand, not "how many letters should be in the first line of my post?" type stuff.

90 minutes, willingness to practice, and some patience.

## Post 2
Date: August 2025 (approx)
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_this-quote-changed-how-i-write-content-forever-activity-7349041780949991426-ATKx

This quote changed how I write content forever:

"People will do anything for those who encourage their dreams, justify their failures, allay their fears, confirm their suspicions, and help them throw rocks at their enemies."

It's by Blair Warren, and he's 100% right.

So I started using this tactic in my posts.
You can too.

Next time you write something, don't say:

"Here's 3 tips to grow your audience"

Say:

"Platforms are rigged for accounts with 100K+ followers.
Meanwhile, talented creators with real expertise get ignored.
Here are 3 ways to change that..."

Same tactical content. But now you're throwing rocks at their enemy.

An "enemy" isn't a specific person. It's an idea.

And it's a key part of improving your engagement overnight.

Here's the framework:

1. Call out what makes them angry
2. Confirm they're not crazy for thinking it
3. Position yourself as their ally against it
4. Then deliver the tactical solution

This is one of the key marketing tactics that helped me build an 8-figure business.

And I documented the entire behind-the-scenes process of building my $10M business.

No fluff. No theory. Just what actually worked.
Stop just consuming.
Start learning what works

## Post 3
Date: August 2025 (approx)
URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_i-published-content-every-single-day-for-activity-7352665582393036801-tS_T

I published content every single day for 8.5 months without getting a single customer.

I followed the playbook.

"Provide value."
"Be consistent."
"Show up daily."

But nothing worked.

People liked my writing.
They even left nice comments.
But no one ever reached out.

It felt pretty damn frustrating.

So I stopped and took a step back.

I pulled the last 50 pieces I wrote and asked one question:

"If I was my ideal customer...would this make me trust the person who wrote it?"

The answer? Most of the time, no.

Here's what I realized:

My writing was vague — it didn't show I understood real problems
My stories were surface level — they didn't build any level of credibility
My CTAs were unclear — I wasn't giving people a reason to act

So I made 3 key changes:

1. I wrote for one person, not "an audience"
2. I shared my experience, not generic help
3. I ended with a simple, useful next step

That's when things changed for me.

I started getting attention and driving demand.

My DMs changed. 
My revenue changed. 
My business changed.

So I turned everything I learned into a system that now powers how I write, post, and sell.

If you’re writing solid content but it’s not turning into trust, leads, or revenue…

This is exactly what I needed back then, and what I use to scale now
