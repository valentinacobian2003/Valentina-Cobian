## Post 2

If I were building a B2B LinkedIn strategy from scratch, here are the 5 systems I'd install first:

1. THE 90-DAY AUTHORITY SYSTEM
Forget "thought leadership." Build proof.
→ Document 1 client win per week (with permission)
→ Share the messy middle, not just the victory lap
→ Tag the client. Let them tell the story.
(This alone generated €2.3M in referrals last year)

2. THE COMMENT-FIRST SYSTEM
Your posts are the side dish. Comments are the main course.
→ 10 strategic comments daily on ideal client posts
→ Add value, don't pitch (ever)
→ Bookmark their content for future reference
(My top 3 clients came from comments, not content)

3. THE DM CONVERSION SYSTEM
Stop sending connection requests with pitches.
→ Connect without a message
→ Engage with 3 posts first
→ Then send a DM about THEIR content, not your services
(67% response rate vs. 3% for pitch messages)

4. THE PROFILE OPTIMIZATION SYSTEM
Your profile is a landing page. Treat it like one.
→ Headline: WHO you help + HOW (not your job title)
→ Featured section: One clear next step
→ About: Their problems first, your solution second
(Profile views → inquiries increased 4.2x)

5. THE WEEKLY AUDIT SYSTEM
What gets measured gets monetized.
→ Track: Profile views, post impressions, DM conversations
→ Identify: Which content drives conversations
→ Double down on what works, kill what doesn't
(Most people post blindly. Data doesn't lie.)

The absolute truth?

You don't need 10K followers.
You don't need viral posts.
You don't need daily content.

You need systems that turn connections into conversations.
And conversations into contracts.

I've helped 1,000+ companies build these systems.
The ones who implement all 5? They're booked solid within 6 months.

Which system are you missing?

Stop asking how often to post.
Start asking how deep your content goes.

I recently helped 2 top entrepreneurs and 2 executives rethink their entire LinkedIn approach.

Same problem across all four:
They were posting daily. Getting less traction. Burning out.

The fix wasn't more content.
It was better alignment.

Here's what we changed:

𝟭. 𝗣𝗿𝗼𝗳𝗶𝗹𝗲 + 𝗖𝗼𝗻𝘁𝗲𝗻𝘁 𝗔𝗹𝗶𝗴𝗻𝗺𝗲𝗻𝘁
We narrowed their focus to 2–3 clear topics.
Their profile reflected those topics.
Their posts reinforced them.

No more random content hoping something sticks.

𝟮. 𝗗𝗲𝗽𝘁𝗵 𝗢𝘃𝗲𝗿 𝗙𝗿𝗲𝗾𝘂𝗲𝗻𝗰𝘆
One strong angle per post.
One clear story.
No filler, no fluff.

The algorithm rewards density now—not volume.

𝟯. 𝗘𝗻𝗴𝗮𝗴𝗲𝗺𝗲𝗻𝘁 𝗧𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗔𝗰𝘁𝘂𝗮𝗹𝗹𝘆 𝗠𝗼𝘃𝗲𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗡𝗲𝗲𝗱𝗹𝗲
They blocked time before and after posting.
Created a tailor-made feed to engage efficiently.
Replied to comments. Started conversations on other profiles.

The algorithm reads that as value.
Their audience feels it as care.

The result?

Reach is still lower than 2023 for all of them.
But leads and real conversations? Up 3x

In 2026, "How often do I post?" is the wrong question.

A better one:
"How do my profile, my content, and my engagement work together?"

That's the system I focus on now.
