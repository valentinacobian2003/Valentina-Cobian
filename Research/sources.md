LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS


The 10 Experts

1. Justin Welsh
Role: Solopreneur, former VP of Sales at PatientPop and ZocDoc
LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/justinwelsh/
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/@JustinWelsh

Justin is probably the clearest proof that LinkedIn content alone can build a real business. He left his VP of Sales role at PatientPop (which scaled to $70M ARR) and built a one person business that crossed seven figures in revenue with no paid ads and no team. He came up with the "zero click content" concept which means writing posts that give full value without linking out anywhere so the algorithm rewards them with more reach. Over 100,000 people have taken his LinkedIn course. He is on this list because his whole career is basically the case study.
Main topics: content systems, zero click content, writing hooks, turning one idea into a week of posts

2. Lara Acosta
Role: LinkedIn personal branding coach and entrepreneur
LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/laraacostar/
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/@Laraacosta

Lara went from unemployed to six figures in under two years using LinkedIn as her only channel. She grew from zero to 300,000 followers and was ranked the number one female creator in the UK. What makes her really useful as a source is how open she is about her process. She shares exactly what she posts, when and why. She created the SLAY framework (Story, Lesson, Action, Your call to action) for writing B2B posts and has proven it works at scale. Her story is also a good reminder that you do not need an existing brand or connections to start because she had neither.
Main topics: SLAY framework, building from zero, posting timing strategy, short form storytelling

3. Jasmin Alic
Role: LinkedIn writing coach and copywriter  
LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/jasmin-alic/  
YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/@JasminAlic  


Jasmin Alic is one of the most precise and tactical voices on LinkedIn writing right now. He built his audience entirely by teaching one thing really well: how to write LinkedIn posts that are impossible to ignore. What makes him different from most writing coaches is that he goes deep into the actual mechanics of a post — sentence structure, pacing, how to open and close, why certain words kill engagement. He is not teaching mindset or personal brand philosophy, he is teaching craft. His YouTube videos are especially useful because he breaks down real posts line by line and explains exactly what is working and why. For a research project about LinkedIn content strategy, having someone who focuses purely on the writing layer is essential and Jasmin is the best person doing that right now.


4. Chris Walker
Role: Founder of Refine Labs, CEO of Passetto
LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/chriswalker171/
Podcast: B2B Revenue Vitals

Chris Walker is one of the most influential people in B2B demand generation and LinkedIn is his main channel. He is the person who pushed the idea that most B2B marketing attribution is broken because buyers do their research in private (Slack, DMs, word of mouth) way before they ever fill out a form and most companies have no idea that is happening. His argument is that LinkedIn organic content is one of the only ways to reach buyers during that invisible research phase. His content is dense and often pretty contrarian which makes it valuable because it challenges the assumptions most SaaS marketers still operate on.
Main topics: demand creation vs demand capture, dark social, why lead gen is broken, LinkedIn as a top of funnel channel

5. Katelyn Bourgoin
Role: Founder of Customer Camp, known as "The Customer Whisperer"
LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/katebour/
Newsletter: Why We Buy (60,000+ subscribers)

Katelyn is a bit different from the others on this list because she is not primarily a LinkedIn growth person. She is a buyer psychology expert. I included her because LinkedIn content strategy is only as good as the message it carries and nobody teaches the messaging layer better than she does. She is a 4x founder who failed a VC backed startup despite doing 300 customer interviews and that experience is what taught her how most marketers fundamentally misunderstand their buyers. Her newsletter Why We Buy applies behavioral science to marketing decisions. You really cannot build a good content strategy without understanding why people pay attention and act in the first place and that is what she teaches.
Main topics: buyer psychology, messaging strategy, why customers say one thing and do another, writing content that connects to real triggers

6. Richard van der Blom
Role: Founder of Just Connecting, LinkedIn algorithm researcher
LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/richardvanderblom/
Annual report: Algorithm Insights Report (2023, 2024 and 2025 editions)

Richard produces the most serious independent research on how the LinkedIn algorithm actually works. His 2025 Algorithm Insights Report analyzed over 1.8 million posts from 58,000 profiles over 12 months which makes it about as close to a science paper on LinkedIn content as you can get. Some of his key findings: organic reach is down about 50% but engagement per post is up 12%, meaning fewer people see content but those who do interact more. Saving a post gives that author an 80% chance of appearing in your feed next time. The first 30 to 60 minutes after posting are critical for reach. Without understanding the algorithm even great content just disappears.
Main topics: how the algorithm decides what to show and to whom, best performing formats in 2025, why timing and early engagement matter

7. Finn Thormeier
Role: Founder of Project 33, host of The Founder Led Marketing Show
LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/finn-thormeier/
Podcast: The Founder Led Marketing Show

Finn runs an agency specifically built to help B2B SaaS founders grow LinkedIn audiences that generate actual pipeline, not just followers. His podcast features SaaS CEOs talking about how they use LinkedIn content as a go to market strategy with guests like Guillaume Moubeche, Chris Walker and Adam Robinson. What is useful about Finn is that he sits right at the intersection of content and revenue. He is not just teaching writing, he is tracking whether the content actually converts. The interviews usually include specific numbers and real experiments which makes them solid research material.
Main topics: founder led marketing, turning LinkedIn engagement into pipeline, SaaS go to market through content

8. Anthony Blatner
Role: Co founder of Speedwork Social, LinkedIn marketing expert
LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/anthonyblatner/

Anthony is the only person on this list focused specifically on the paid side of LinkedIn but that is exactly why he is here. Most LinkedIn content advice treats organic and paid as completely separate things when for B2B SaaS companies trying to scale they really are not. Speedwork Social is one of the only agencies focused exclusively on LinkedIn advertising. He was selected for LinkedIn's 2026 global influencer program. His posts include real numbers, real experiments and honest takes on what works and what does not which is rarer than it should be in this space.
Main topics: organic and paid strategy together, audience targeting for B2B SaaS, writing ad copy that actually performs

9. Ann Handley
Role: Chief Content Officer at MarketingProfs, author of Everybody Writes
LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/annhandley/
Newsletter: Total Annarchy

Ann Handley is the most foundational name on this list. She was the first person anywhere to hold the title Chief Content Officer and her book Everybody Writes is one of the most referenced books in B2B content marketing. I included her because LinkedIn content without strong writing is basically just noise and Ann is the clearest teacher of that craft for B2B audiences. Her own posts demonstrate what she teaches: sharp, warm, full of personality and never generic. She is a useful counterweight to the more tactical growth focused creators on this list because good strategy means nothing if the actual writing does not land.
Main topics: B2B writing craft, finding a human voice in corporate content, building a consistent tone on LinkedIn

10. Adam Robinson
Role: CEO of Retention.com and RB2B
LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/retentionadam/

Adam Robinson is one of the most transparent SaaS founders active on LinkedIn right now. He has built two SaaS companies with LinkedIn as the primary distribution channel and posts about the process in real time including revenue milestones, growth experiments, things that did not work and channel decisions. He has been very open that LinkedIn organic content is how he acquires customers and builds credibility. Unlike many people who teach strategy in the abstract, Adam is showing his work while it is actually happening. That makes his content some of the most honest and useful research material on this topic.
Main topics: building in public, LinkedIn as a SaaS customer acquisition channel, founder credibility as distribution, real numbers and real experiments
